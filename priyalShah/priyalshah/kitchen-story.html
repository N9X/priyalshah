REDESIGN

Kitchen Story

My work for Kitchen Story was to give it a complete makeover by giving it a new branding, making a grid structure, and make the elements readabale and discoverable, and navigable.

Overview

After 9 successful restaurants, Kitchen Story is Steven Choi's 10th new place. While some of his restaurants showcases Thai influences, Kitchen Story “is Californian with a touch of Korean.” There are numerous good reviews on Yelp, Open Table, and Google, about the food. People are willing to wait as much as 40mins in line to get a chance to eat there.

Duration

2 Months

Role

UX researcher, UX designer, Interaction Designer

Team

Solo

Tools

Figma, Invision

Define

Scope

The current website lacks a precise grid structure, making it incredibly difficult to organize elements and leaving large blank spaces throughout the site, making the layout appear incomplete. Since the buttons and links are not prominent, the site is difficult to read. As there is no clear heading or obvious separator, it is challenging to navigate the website. Everything appears to be related and difficult to traverse.

Problem Definition

The goal is to improve the user experience by simplifying and arranging it to make it more intuitive and easy to use and make it faster for users to find what they're searching for and achieve their objectives while expressing the brands' values.

RESEARCH

SWOT Analysis

Strenghts

  • The food is delicious
  • Ambience is nice
  • Interesting menu
  • Friendly staff
  • Usually packed with customers
  • Great reviews

Weaknesses

  • No proper navigation
  • A bit expensive
  • Poor content
  • Inconsistent spacing
  • Fonts difficult to read
  • Overly crowded venue
  • Difficult to get a reservation

Opportunities

  • Engagement on social media
  • Can add customer reviews on the website
  • Add information for online ordering
  • Creating an about us page to give an opportunity to the users to get to know the company better

Threats

  • Many similar restaurants as many restaurants sell fusion cuisine
  • Due to pandemic, food material prices are going up which may cause more expensive dishes
  • Big restaurants are giving out discounts to attract customers

EMPATHIZE

User Personas

After researching the business, I then started researching the users and visitors of Kitchen Story. I looked through Yelp, Open table, and google reviews to narrow down their major user groups. From over 722 reviews on Open Table alone, I was able to understand the age range of the visitor and what they liked or disliked about Kitchen Story. Reading through the reviews gave me insight on what are some pain-points and what users prefer. Using this information, I have created three personas: Graduate Student, Working Professional, and a local business man.

BRANDING

Moodboard

Before jumping to the high fidelity mockups, I decided to explore some sites, photos, patterns, and typography and creaetd a moodboard to help me plan the whole interface.

Logo

Kitchen Story is a family owned restaurant. To represent that, I used a spoon and fork to form a roof and four little squares to represent windows. The logo is based on the Closure property to give a perception of a house. The typeface used is Berkshire Swash. The aim was to give logo a classy and sophisticated look. Berkshire Swash is alluring semi-sweet typestyle with a bold yet feminine flair to it which sets perfectly for my logo goal.
